Viking Ship Assessment

Project Description

The GCIEL Viking Longship VR Experience project aims to revolutionize educational approaches in the 21st century by leveraging virtual reality to create an engaging and immersive learning environment. This experience allows users to actively participate in constructing a Viking longship, enhancing their understanding of historical craftsmanship and culture through interactive storytelling. Each component of the ship is introduced with a unique poem and audio description, offering insights into its significance before users proceed to place the piece as part of the overall assembly.

The Viking Ship Assessment app supports this VR experience by providing an in-depth analysis of player interactions. Designed to capture and visualize engagement, the app focuses on critical aspects such as user position within the VR space, time spent interacting with each ship piece, and the frequency the user views each component. By transforming and displaying this data, the app enables researchers to identify which elements of the VR experience successfully capture and maintain user attention, as well as areas where adjustments might enhance the educational impact.

A primary feature of the Viking Ship Assessment app is the "Piece View Amount" animation, found above. This animation tracks how often each ship component is viewed across users, highlighting potential drop-off points where interest might drop. This insight allows for targeted adjustments to specific pieces that might require additional visual detail or interactivity to retain student interest.

To further explore user engagement, the assessment app examines "Piece Completion Time," capturing how long users spend on each component. This metric is crucial in identifying parts of the experience that might be too complex or, conversely, too simple to hold attention. The experience gives the student the opportunity to view poems that include the name of each component and learn the history behind them. As such this metric can help determine where learning may drop between pieces and what may draw more attention.
